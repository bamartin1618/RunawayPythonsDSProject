The Effect of the COVID-19 Pandemic on The Global Economy

By Kenny Patel, Aiden Yang, Anish Kokal, MJ, Ethan Mader, and Blake Martin

Our project studied a dataset of countries’ number of cases, deaths, GDP, HDI, etc. throughout the COVID-19 pandemic. We wanted to determine to what extent the COVID-19 pandemic impacted the state of the global economy.

Our hypothesis is that the COVID-19 pandemic had an overall negative effect on the global economy.

We created a pie chart to represent the total cases by location for the entire world with each pie slice representing the percentage of total coronavirus cases that originated in that country.

We created this because it gives us a good idea of where most of the COVID cases were diagnosed. It also shows us which countries were the least affected.

We found out that more than half of the total COVID cases in the world came from 4 countries, the USA (24.9%), India (13.1%), Brazil (10.5%), and Russia (4.44%). The reason for this is because these are the highest population countries in the world, therefore meaning that they would have a higher count of coronavirus cases.

One of the main parts about our research was actually what effect do pandemics actually have on our economy. We calculated this by making a line plot about the days into the pandemic to the GDP per capita.

GDP (Gross Domestic Product) is most commonly used to measure the size of an economy.

We found that pandemics have a heavy effect on our economies. If you look at our line graph within the first 50 days we already saw a major collapse when the GDP fell from nearly 33 thousand all the way to 29 thousand. Closing into 150 days the economy GDP fell to 19 thousand. By this we can conclude that pandemic heavily affects the GDP per capita therefore hurting the economy. Thank You!

Our findings support our hypothesis. Our data indicated a negative relationship between the number of days into the pandemic and the average GDP of a country, indicating that the COVID-19 pandemic had an overall negative effect on the global economy. In addition, we created a geo graph showing the HDI of countries during the pandemic.
